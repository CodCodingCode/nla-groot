\begin{abstract}
Vision--language--action (VLA) policies such as NVIDIA's GR00T expose a
high-dimensional Qwen3-VL backbone hidden state \(\hvec\) that is causally
upstream of the action head, yet there is no agreed-upon way to read
\(\hvec\) out as natural language or to write back into it from
language. We adapt the recently proposed Natural Language Autoencoder
(NLA) recipe~\citep{frasertaliente2026nla} to this setting and release
\code{nla-groot}, an open implementation that (i) extracts per-token
activations from GR00T's backbone, (ii) trains a paired \emph{activation
verbalizer} \AV{} (\(\hvec \to y\)) and \emph{activation reconstructor}
\AR{} (\(y \to \hhat\)) with joint supervised fine-tuning plus an optional
GRPO phase that uses \AR{} as a learned reconstruction reward, and (iii)
injects \AR{}'s output back into the live policy server as a backbone
steering perturbation in LIBERO closed-loop simulation. We propose a
three-axis evaluation harness---offline reconstruction, multimodal LLM
grounding judge, and closed-loop steerability A/B---and report a
\emph{negative-result-rich} pilot on LIBERO Goal: our V3 checkpoint
attains a healthy retrieval margin (\(0.124\)) and \(100\%\) caption
\emph{appropriateness} but only \(8.3\%\) anti-template specificity,
\(41.7\%\) grounding, and a zero correct-minus-wrong steering gap on the
single tested task. Reconstruction-only metrics would have called this a
success; the harness does not. We argue this evaluation pattern, and the
``shorthand template collapse'' failure mode it surfaces, are a
prerequisite for any future NLA-style interpretability work on VLA
policies.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Recent vision--language--action (VLA) models pair a Vision-Language
backbone with a diffusion or flow-matching action head and act
end-to-end on physical robots~\citep{nvidia2025gr00t,
brohan2023rt2,kim2024openvla}. As capabilities scale, an old question
returns in a new form: \emph{what does the model \textbf{think} it is
about to do, expressed in a representation a human can audit and
edit?} Mechanistic interpretability tooling for language
models---probes~\citep{alain2017linear,belinkov2022probing}, sparse
autoencoders~\citep{cunningham2023sparseautoencoders,bricken2023monosemanticity},
and activation steering~\citep{turner2023actadd,zou2023repe}---has not
yet been adapted to the policy setting, where downstream behavior is
itself an evaluation modality.

\paragraph{The NLA recipe, on a VLA.}
\citet{frasertaliente2026nla} showed that an LM's hidden state can be
``round-tripped'' through natural language: a small \emph{activation
verbalizer} (\AV) is trained to caption an injected activation, and a
paired \emph{activation reconstructor} (\AR) is trained to invert the
caption back into activation space, providing both an interpretability
artifact (the caption) and a causal handle (the reconstructed vector).
This paper asks whether the same recipe transfers to a VLA backbone,
where the hidden state is intended to carry scene-and-task semantics
that the action head consumes.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[topsep=2pt,itemsep=2pt,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{System:} an end-to-end open implementation
    (\code{nla-groot}) that extracts GR00T backbone activations
    (Qwen3-VL, 16-layer truncation, \(d{=}2048\)), labels them with a
    multimodal teacher, jointly SFT-trains \AV{}/\AR{} on top of a
    Qwen3-4B-Instruct base, and exposes \AR{}'s output as a live
    backbone steering vector in the official GR00T LIBERO policy
    server (\S\ref{sec:method}).
  \item \textbf{Evaluation harness:} three complementary axes---offline
    reconstruction (FVE/cosine/retrieval), multimodal LLM judge on
    cached frames (axes B/C), and closed-loop sim A/B with matched-,
    mismatched-, and null-caption arms---wired into a single PASS/WARN/FAIL
    \emph{scorecard}~(\S\ref{sec:eval}).
  \item \textbf{Pilot findings:} on a LIBERO 4-suite combined corpus,
    our best checkpoint passes reconstruction-style metrics but
    \emph{fails} grounding and steering. We characterize this
    ``shorthand template collapse'' failure mode, show that retrieval
    margin and reconstruction cosine alone are insufficient as a
    workshop-grade success criterion, and surface a train--deploy
    distribution gap that limits behavioral
    steering~(\S\ref{sec:results}).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Thesis.} \emph{NLA-style autoencoding is a credible
direction for VLA interpretability, but only behind a multi-axis
harness; reconstruction metrics in isolation are misleading.}

\section{Background and related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Vision--language--action policies.}
GR00T~\citep{nvidia2025gr00t} couples a Cosmos-Reason2-2B / Qwen3-VL
backbone with a diffusion action head; only the first
\code{SELECT\_LAYER}\(=\!16\) decoder layers are retained at deployment,
so the policy effectively reads a 2048-d hidden state at layer 16.
Related VLAs include RT-2~\citep{brohan2023rt2},
OpenVLA~\citep{kim2024openvla}, and Octo~\citep{octo2024}. We focus on
GR00T because its backbone--head split is explicit and its checkpoints
are public.

\paragraph{Activation interpretability.}
Linear probes recover task-relevant subspaces~\citep{alain2017linear};
sparse autoencoders~\citep{cunningham2023sparseautoencoders,
bricken2023monosemanticity, templeton2024scaling} factor activations
into monosemantic features. NLA~\citep{frasertaliente2026nla}
complements these by producing \emph{free-text} explanations
\(y=\AV(\hvec)\) and a faithfulness anchor
\(\hhat=\AR(y)\). \code{nla-groot} ports the recipe to a VLA backbone.

\paragraph{Activation steering.}
Activation editing has demonstrated controllable behavior in LMs via
linear directions~\citep{turner2023actadd, zou2023repe,
liu2024incontext, panickssery2024steering}. In robotics, behavioral
steering is rarer; closed-loop sim is the appropriate validation modality
when the model is a policy. We treat \AR{}'s output as a candidate
steering vector and \emph{measure} whether it changes downstream
behavior on LIBERO.

\paragraph{Robotic simulation benchmarks.}
LIBERO~\citep{liu2023libero} is a suite of MuJoCo-based manipulation
tasks with public assets; GR00T ships posttrained checkpoints per LIBERO
sub-suite (e.g.\ \code{libero\_goal}). We use these as the closed-loop
ground truth.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\subsection{Data: extraction and labeling}

Per-token backbone activations are captured by a forward hook on the
GR00T \code{backbone\_features} tensor, before the post-VL LayerNorm and
the cross-attention to the action head (canonicalized in
\code{src/nla/layer\_spec.py}). For each LeRobot episode and step we
record \(\hvec_i \in \mathbb{R}^{2048}\) at three position types (text,
image-patch, image-summary; see~\citet{frasertaliente2026nla}). A
multimodal teacher (GPT-class) is shown the raw camera frame(s),
instruction, and token-position metadata, and emits a structured
bullet-point caption that we treat as gold for SFT.

A per-corpus scalar \(\alpha\) is estimated as the 75th percentile of
\(\|\hvec\|_2\) and used for both volumetric calibration of injection
and as the unit of MSE in \AR{} training. For the LIBERO 4-suite combined
corpus \(\alpha \approx 203.98\).

\subsection{Architecture: \AV{} and \AR{}}

Both \AV{} and \AR{} are LoRA fine-tunes (rank 32, \(\alpha=64\), full
Qwen attention/MLP target set, bfloat16) of \code{Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507}.

\paragraph{\AV{}: activation \(\to\) text.}
The prompt template contains a single \code{<|act\_slot|>} token. At
forward time we tokenize the prompt, embed the IDs, then overwrite the
slot embedding with \(\alpha \cdot \mathrm{normalize}(\mathbf{W}_p
\hvec)\) where \(\mathbf{W}_p \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times 2048}\) is a
learnable projector. Loss is cross-entropy on the gold bullet caption.

\paragraph{\AR{}: text \(\to\) activation.}
The base LM is physically truncated to its first 16 layers (matching
GR00T's \code{SELECT\_LAYER}). A linear head reads the last non-pad
hidden state of the prompt
``\emph{Summary of the following text:}~\(\langle\)text\(\rangle\)\emph{
\(\langle\)summary\(\rangle\)}''
and predicts \(\hhat / \alpha \in \mathbb{R}^{2048}\). Training loss is
mean-squared error in \(\alpha\)-scaled space, plus an InfoNCE
contrastive term with mined hard negatives (top-\(k\) cosine, \(k{=}4\)).

\subsection{Training}

\paragraph{Joint SFT.}
\AV{} and \AR{} are updated on the same batches, so \AR{} sees \AV{}'s
gold-text targets and \AV{} cannot drift independently. The headline
recipe (run \code{libero\_4suite\_v3}) uses 15{,}000 steps at
batch~\(=\!4\), lr~\(=\!10^{-4}\), 500-step warmup, cosine schedule,
\(\mathrm{ar\_av\_mix\_max}=0.4\) (scheduled AR-on-\AV{}-sampled-text
mixing past 30\% of training to close the train--deploy text gap), and
held-out fraction~\(=\!0.05\) episode-split.

\paragraph{Optional GRPO phase.}
After SFT, \AV{} can be further trained by GRPO with the frozen \AR{} as
the reward model: for each \(\hvec\) we sample \(K\) captions, score
each by \(-\|\hvec/\alpha - \AR(y)\|^2\), and apply a within-group
advantage policy gradient with a Schulman-k3 KL to a frozen reference
\AV{}.

\subsection{Steering: language \(\to\) live policy}

At inference, a user supplies a short bullet caption \(y\). We compute
\(\hhat=\AR(y)\) and inject it into the live GR00T policy server by
replacing or blending the backbone token features at one of three
placements: a single \code{image\_patch} token, \emph{all} image-patch
tokens (\code{image\_patch\_all}), or the image-summary token. A scalar
\code{blend}\(\in[0,2]\) controls intensity. The injection is wired
through \code{src/nla/steering/sim\_policy\_wrapper.py} so the official
LIBERO sim client sees no difference apart from modified backbone
features on every \code{get\_action} call.

\section{Evaluation harness}
\label{sec:eval}

We deliberately decline to call any one number ``the score''. Instead,
each checkpoint is graded on three axes; a single scorecard
(\code{scripts/eval/build\_v3\_scorecard.py}) emits PASS/WARN/FAIL per
metric against pre-registered thresholds.

\subsection{Axis 1 -- Offline reconstruction and discriminability}

\begin{itemize}[topsep=2pt,itemsep=2pt,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Closed-loop cosine}: \(\cos(\hvec,
    \AR(\AV(\hvec)))\) (rather than teacher-forced), to penalize \AV{}
    captions that \AR{} cannot invert.
  \item \textbf{Retrieval margin} and \textbf{retrieval@\(K\)}: matched
    vs.\ cross-pair cosine on \(\AR(\AV(\hvec))\) against a held-out
    pool; catches non-discriminative \(\hhat\) (template collapse).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Axis 2 -- Multimodal LLM judge}

\code{scripts/eval/llm\_judge\_av\_captions.py} re-renders the original
frame, hands the multimodal judge the \emph{frame} + caption, and
scores three rubrics: (A)~format/length, (B)~\emph{grounding} (does the
caption describe what is in the picture, not a generic template), and
(C)~\emph{appropriateness} (is it the right kind of caption for this
position type). Gold (teacher) captions on the same frames give a
calibration ceiling; we report both gold and \AV{} pass rates and their
gap.

\subsection{Axis 3 -- Closed-loop steerability A/B}

Hardware-grounded: a steerability A/B (\code{scripts/eval/closed\_loop\_sim\_ab.py}
and \code{scripts/eval/steerability\_eval.py}) runs LIBERO Goal episodes
on the official GR00T checkpoint under three caption arms per task:
\emph{baseline} (no steer), \emph{correct} (caption that matches the
scene/instruction), and \emph{wrong} (a contradictory caption). The
headline number is \(\Delta_{cw} = \mathrm{succ}_{\text{correct}} -
\mathrm{succ}_{\text{wrong}}\); we target \(\Delta_{cw} \geq +5\)pp as
PASS. Per-object displacement and minimum end-effector distance are
logged for failure analysis.

\subsection{Closed-loop steerability}

Across three steer prompts \(\times\) two AR variants
(\code{libero\_goal\_pilot} and \code{libero\_4suite\_v3}) \(\times\) 3
seeds (Table~\ref{tab:steer}, distilled from
\code{data/eval/steerability\_v1\_vs\_v3/metrics.json}), the baseline
policy solves the task on every seed
(\code{success\_any\_rate}\(=1.0\)) and every steering condition
\emph{stops} it from doing so (\code{success\_any\_rate}\(=0.0\)). This
is a robust behavioral effect---steer-on does change behavior---but it
is symmetric: the correct (\code{bowl\_plate}) and contradictory
(\code{wine\_rack}, \code{bowl\_stove}) prompts both block the task,
giving \(\Delta_{cw}=0\) and disqualifying any semantic claim from this
pilot. The \emph{per-object} displacement and end-effector traces
(Figure~\ref{fig:displacement}) reveal that all steered conditions
collapse the bowl's per-object displacement to \(\approx 0.07\)m versus
\(0.145\)m at baseline---i.e.\ steering reduces motion uniformly rather
than redirecting it to the prompted object.

\subsection{Reading the results}

Three observations dominate.

\paragraph{(i) Reconstruction passes; behavior does not.}
The retrieval margin and appropriateness rate would, on their own,
read as a successful training run. The judge's grounding and
anti-template scores, and the symmetric LIBERO-Goal failure, contradict
that read directly. This validates the harness: had we shipped only the
reconstruction-style metrics we would have over-claimed.

\paragraph{(ii) Shorthand template collapse persists in V3.}
The 8.3\% anti-template rate on the 4-suite holdout means \(\AV{}\)
captions are mostly indistinguishable from a corpus-mean template. This
is the same failure mode reported for the earlier
\code{droid\_100ep\_v2\_nce} run (V2 post-mortem,
\code{docs/sft\_plan/06\_v2\_postmortem\_v3\_rerun.md}); V3's mitigations
(\code{ar\_av\_mix\_max}, InfoNCE hard negatives, episode-split)
narrowed the gap but did not close it.

\paragraph{(iii) Steering bites, but is not yet semantic.}
Every steered condition blocks task completion---so there is energy in
the perturbation---but the prompt's identity does not appear to choose
\emph{what} the policy attends to. We attribute this to (a) the
AR's train--deploy distribution gap (it is trained on gold-style
bullet captions and deployed on hand-written ones), and (b) the
\code{image\_patch\_all} placement at blend~\(=\!1.0\) being a coarse
intervention (consistent with the open-loop probe in
\code{scripts/eval/nla\_steer\_quant\_probe.py}).

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\begin{itemize}[topsep=2pt,itemsep=2pt,leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Single task, small \(n\).} The closed-loop sim numbers
    above are three seeds on one LIBERO Goal task. We frame them as
    feasibility, not a benchmark; a multi-task LIBERO sweep with
    per-prompt affordance scoring is required before any quantitative
    semantic-steering claim.
  \item \textbf{Teacher--$\hvec$ confound.} The multimodal teacher sees
    the original frame and may mention scene facts that are only
    weakly present in \(\hvec\), so SFT loss does not strictly equal
    ``what \(\hvec\) encodes''. This is inherited from the NLA recipe
    and motivates the judge's grounding axis.
  \item \textbf{AR train--deploy gap.} \AR{} is trained on gold-style
    bullet captions; user-written steer prompts are out of
    distribution. The \code{ar\_av\_mix\_max} schedule reduces but
    does not eliminate this gap.
  \item \textbf{Single backbone, single layer.} Activations are
    extracted at layer 16 (\code{SELECT\_LAYER}) before the
    post-VL LayerNorm. Other choices (\code{vl\_embeds},
    DiT mid-block) are documented in \code{src/nla/layer\_spec.py} but
    not ablated here.
  \item \textbf{Judge as oracle.} The grounding judge is itself a large
    multimodal model and inherits its own miscalibrations; we mitigate
    by pairing every \AV{} score with a gold score on identical
    frames.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion and outlook}
\label{sec:discussion}

\code{nla-groot} establishes that the NLA recipe is implementable on a
production VLA backbone and that the resulting checkpoint can be put
in the loop of a public closed-loop simulator. The principal
contribution of this short paper is the harness, not the headline
number: a multi-axis scorecard with pre-registered thresholds and a
behavioral arm is, in our experience, what separates an apparently
successful run from an actually successful one.

A natural next step is the GRPO phase against the AR-as-reward signal,
which we have plumbed (\code{scripts/training/run\_grpo.py}) but do not
report here. We expect GRPO with judge-blended reward to attack the
template-collapse mode head-on, since judge rewards penalize generic
captions in a way the supervised cross-entropy does not.
