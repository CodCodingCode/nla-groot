v9 — Reconstruction-quality lever for the codec
=================================================

v9 = v8 + (no AR truncation) + (magnitude-aware reconstruction loss).

It targets a specific pathology v8 ended in, not a fundamentally new
architecture or new data:

  v8 final (step 5600, mid-run snapshot used here):
    val MSE        ≈ 350  (raw activation-space units)
    val cosine     ≈ 0.526
    val FVE        ≈ -82
    closed_greedy/cosine ≈ 0.556
    closed_greedy/fve    ≈ -76

The "high cosine + deeply negative FVE" signature means: the codec
captures *direction* in activation space well enough, but the *magnitude*
of each reconstructed vector is systematically wrong. Plain MSE training
can't fix this on its own because direction error and magnitude error
both contribute to MSE with the same gradient sign — the optimizer can't
tell which is the bigger problem and tends to anchor on the easier-to-
optimize component (direction here).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Why we kept truncate_to_n_layers=16 through v8

Originally a memory + speed constraint, not a principled architectural
choice. Activation extraction reads layer-16 hidden states from GR00T's
Cosmos-Reason2-2B backbone (a Qwen3-VL truncated at layer 16). The
*targets* live at "layer 16 semantic depth." The AR is a *separate*
Qwen3-4B-Instruct model whose job is text -> 2048-d vector matching that
target — its transformer depth is a free hyperparameter, not tied to
anything about the target.

Truncating AR to 16 layers was inherited from v3 — likely the original
authors needed memory budget for the AR + AV + frozen policy + DiT all
on one H100. It became default and no one revisited it.

The capacity-matching argument ("AR depth should mirror target source
depth") is hand-wavy. The AR doesn't need to share semantic level with
its target; it needs to be expressive enough to map text -> target.
Full Qwen3-4B has roughly twice the layers and twice the LoRA-eligible
modules, giving the head more capacity to refine its reconstruction.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Why decomposed loss specifically

The relevant math:

  MSE(p, t)  =  ||p - t||²
              =  ||p||² + ||t||² - 2 ||p|| ||t|| cos(p, t)
              =  ||p||² + ||t||² - 2 ||p|| ||t|| (1 - dir_loss)
              =  (||p|| - ||t||)² + 2 ||p|| ||t|| dir_loss
                        ────────         ──────────────────
                        magnitude          direction

So plain MSE *does* contain both terms — but multiplicatively coupled.
The gradient with respect to ||p|| depends on both the magnitude error
and the direction error simultaneously. When direction is bad, the
optimizer can't push magnitude into the right neighborhood without
making direction worse. When direction is OK (cosine ~0.55), the
gradient on magnitude is damped by the cosine term and progress slows.

Decomposing the loss as:

  loss = (1 - cosine(p, t)).mean()
       + ar_scale_weight * (log||p|| - log||t||)².mean()

removes this coupling. The direction term is a pure cosine cost (no
||p|| or ||t|| in the gradient). The magnitude term is a log-norm-ratio
cost (scale-invariant; over- and under-shoot equally penalized). The
two terms can be weighted independently via ar_scale_weight, letting
us crank up the magnitude signal until norms come into line.

This is the simplest possible intervention that addresses the symptom
without changing what the model has to predict.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. What we expect v9 to move

Predicted direction of change (not magnitudes):

  Metric                        v8 final     v9 expected     reason
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  val cosine                    0.526        0.50-0.55       slight drop OK (we de-prioritize this)
  val MSE (raw units)           350          150-250         magnitude term tightens norms
  val FVE                       -82          -10 to +0.1     consequence of MSE drop
  closed_greedy cosine          0.556        0.55-0.60       not the target metric for v9
  closed_greedy MSE             347          120-220         the headline v9 metric
  closed_greedy FVE             -76          -10 to +0.2     headline v9 metric
  steer_lift_predicate          unknown      +0.05 to +0.20  IF magnitude was the real bottleneck
  step time                     ~6 s         ~9-12 s         from full AR depth

The cosine "slight drop OK" is important: a small decrease in cosine
in exchange for substantially better FVE/MSE is a win, because for
*steering* the magnitudes need to be approximately right so the
injected vector competes correctly with the live activation
distribution at the patch positions. v8 had vectors with the wrong
norm, which the policy may interpret as "small effect" or "outlier
direction," ignoring the codec.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

4. What v9 does NOT fix

- Caption quality (whether AV produces intent-distinguishing text). v9
  inherits the v6 labels and AV recipe; if captions are still generic
  scene descriptions, v9 reconstructs them faithfully but the codec is
  still inert. Caption audit should run independently.
- The truncate=0 + decomposed-loss change does not introduce new
  trainable capacity that helps with *semantics* — it helps with
  reconstruction faithfulness on the already-learned semantic content.
- Sim eval protocol: --sim-max-steps=100 leaves nearly all BDDL
  predicates at 0% success even on baseline runs. r_sim is the only
  measurable signal at that horizon. Bump to 200-300 for the v9 CF
  eval if predicates are the headline.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

5. Timing

Launch only after v8_full_6400 + its CF eval has finished. Two reasons:

(a) v9 needs the GPU. With v8 still running, a parallel v9 run on the
    same H100 will OOM.
(b) v9 is a one-axis change against v8. Knowing v8's CF eval number
    tells us whether magnitude really is the bottleneck, or whether v9
    is fixing the wrong thing.

v9 is expected to take ~18-22 hours at 6400 steps with the canonical
recipe in v9_runbook.md.
